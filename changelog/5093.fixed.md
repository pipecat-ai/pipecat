- Fixed word-timestamp TTS services (e.g. Cartesia) writing the second turn's text to the assistant/transcript context twice on back-to-back (superseding) LLM responses with no user turn between them, even though the audio was synthesized once. When the second turn's context tore down before its word timestamps arrived, `force_complete()` emitted the turn's text and emptied the sequencer queue, then the late words fell through to the passthrough path and re-emitted the whole turn. `AggregatedFrameSequencer` now tracks contexts it has force-completed and drops later word-timestamps for them.
